Institutional Challenge

The IT Department of Nepal Rastra Bank was operating with fragmented, paper-based legal workflows that created significant operational and compliance risks. Key issues included slow approval cycles (7–10 days per case), lack of bilingual (English/Nepali) support, and no audit-ready activity logs. This created audit exposure and governance inefficiencies at a central banking level.

Project Scope

The objective was to modernize NRB’s legal operations by replacing paper-based processes with a centralized, secure digital governance platform. The scope included:

  • End-to-End Case Management: Lifecycle tracking from initiation to resolution.
  • Maker–Checker Workflow: Enforcement of the 4-Eye Principle for every legal action.
  • Role-Based Access Control: Structured permissions for Creators, Checkers, and Admins.
  • Document Management: Encrypted file storage with secure intranet deployment.
  • Bilingual Legal System: Full English and Nepali Unicode support with Dual Date (BS/AD).
  • Immutable Audit Trail: Permanent logging of every action for regulatory transparency.

Our Engineering Approach

1.

Governance-First Architecture

System designed directly from central bank compliance and audit requirements to ensure institutional trust.

2.

Risk-Eliminating Workflows

Implementation of Maker-Checker enforcement to ensure zero unauthorized legal actions within the system.

3.

Fully isolated deployment within the NRB internal network ensuring absolute data sovereignty and security.

4.
